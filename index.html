Sports

Palermo is home to the SSD Palermo soccer team, which was founded 120 years ago. Palermo is also home to the University Sports Center of Palermo, which has average 1,000 people per day

History

Palermo was founded in the 8th century BC. It has since been under control of different empires, including Rome, The Arabs, and Normans, just to name a few.

Transportation

With Palermo on the coast of Sicily, it is easy to get there via boat. If that isn't your thing, then you can also travel via plane, car, train, or bus.

Influence

Palermo is a very important city to the rest of the world, and serves as a time capsule of the great empires that once ruled it. Palermo was nicknamed the "most conquered city" because of how many empires it has come in contact with.

Places to Visit

Here are some attractions in Palermo that will not disappoint.

Palermo Cathedral

Due to the fact that Palermo has been ruled by several different nations and empires, the Palermo cathedral has been altered by each, and therefore has several different styles, which is truly a fascinating site.

Capella Palatina

The Capella Palatina is in the center of Palermo, and is one of the best preserved examples of Byzantine architecture and artwork in Europe. It was built in 1132, and is covered in fascinating artwork and arcitecture.

Palazzo dei Normanni

The Palazzo dei Normanni served as the seat of the Kings of Sicily. The Palazzo dei Normanni is one of the oldest royal palaces in all of Europe, and was created in the 9th century.

Palermo Archaeological Museum

The Palermo Archaeological Museum is the perfect spot for people who love history. The museum is full of ancient Greek art and artefacts from the island of Sicily.
